SECTION 4

Transhumance

Transhumance refers to the seasonal movement of people with their livestock between fixed summer and winter pastures. There are two main types of transhumance: fixed and nomadic.

Fixed transhumance is practiced mainly in countries with predictable seasons, such as Switzerland, while nomadic transhumance occurs in areas with unpredictable climatic conditions, like Somalia. These two types differ significantly in their patterns of movement, living arrangements, and social organization.

The study of pastoralist movements reveals distinct patterns for each type. Fixed transhumance follows a regular, predictable route, while nomadic transhumance involves more complex and varied movements.

Differences are also evident in terms of housing structures, the number of people involved in the migrations, and economic practices. Research in Algeria has uncovered specific details about local transhumance practices, including timing, shelter types, and appropriate terminology.
